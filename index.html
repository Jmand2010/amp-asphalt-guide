Executive Summary

This document provides a professional-grade comparison of asphalt pavement systems using Wisconsin mix designs and regional best practices.

Critical Truth: Up to 70% of pavement performance is controlled by base and subgrade — not asphalt thickness alone.

Wisconsin Mix Designs

4LT 58-28S

  • Coarse aggregate (~1/2")
  • Higher structural strength
  • Lower permeability
  • Best for binder/base layers

5LT 58-28S

  • Finer aggregate (~3/8")
  • Smoother finish
  • Higher workability
  • Best for surface layer

30-Year Pavement Lifecycle

  • 0–5 Years → Excellent
  • 5–10 Years → Minor cracking
  • 10–15 Years → Maintenance critical
  • 15–25 Years → Overlay phase
  • 25–30 Years → Reconstruction
Early maintenance saves 4–5x in long-term cost.

Why Cheap Asphalt Costs More

Thin pavement systems fail rapidly due to structural fatigue and moisture infiltration. While initial costs are lower, repeated replacement cycles result in significantly higher lifecycle cost.

Investing in proper thickness and structure reduces long-term expenses and improves performance.

Engineering Disclaimer

This document is for informational purposes only. Pavement performance depends on subgrade conditions, drainage, traffic loading, and construction practices. Final design should be verified by a qualified professional.
